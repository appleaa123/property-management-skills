# Universal Form Workflow Details

Legal notices are the most highly scrutinized documents in property management. This 6-step process ensures compliance and minimizes liability.

## 1. Detection
*   **Why it matters legally**: Serving the wrong form type (e.g., an "end of tenancy" form instead of a "cure or quit" form) automatically voids the legal process in almost all jurisdictions.
*   **Action**: Always verify the specific form required for the exact situation in the specific region.

## 2. Gathering
*   **Why it matters legally**: Misspelling a tenant's name or getting the address wrong can result in a judge throwing out an eviction case months later.
*   **Action**: Pull exact strings from the database. Do not abbreviate or summarize.

## 3. Gap-Filling (The Interview)
*   **Why it matters legally**: Forms require specific facts (dates, times, dollar amounts, specific behavioral descriptions). The database only holds static data; the manager holds the situational facts.
*   **Action**: If a form requires "Details of the damage," ask the manager: "Please describe exactly what the tenant damaged and when it was discovered so I can add it to the form."

## 4. Drafting
*   **Why it matters legally**: Many jurisdictions provide mandatory PDF or template structures.
*   **Action**: Ensure the generated draft matches the required sections of the official form.

## 5. Approval (HITL)
*   **Why it matters legally**: The manager acts as the legal agent of the owner. They must review the form for factual accuracy before it becomes a binding communication.
*   **Action**: Present the full text. Wait for a "Yes" or "Approved."

## 6. Delivery & Audit
*   **Why it matters legally**: Proof of service is required to enforce any notice.
*   **Action**: Advise the manager on legally acceptable delivery methods for that specific form (e.g., "This form cannot be emailed; you must tape it to the door"). Always log the action.
