# Rent Control Policy Research Guide

Rent control laws vary significantly by jurisdiction and change frequently. **You must always search for the most up-to-date information.**

When researching a new jurisdiction, extract these four key data points:
1.  **Guideline Percentage**: The maximum legal % increase for the current calendar year.
2.  **Notice Period**: The exact number of days required before the increase takes effect (often 30, 60, or 90 days).
3.  **Frequency Limit**: How often rent can be increased (usually once per 12 months).
4.  **Exemptions**: Rules that void the rent control limits (e.g., buildings built after a specific year, single-family homes).

## Known Policy Cache (Examples)

Below are examples of how policy data is typically structured. **Do not assume these figures are currently accurate; verify them.**

**Ontario, Canada**
*   **Guideline**: Set annually by the government (e.g., 2.5%).
*   **Notice Period**: 90 Days.
*   **Exemptions**: Buildings occupied for the first time after Nov 15, 2018, are exempt from the guideline limit.
*   **Required Form**: Form N1 (Standard) or Form N2 (Exempt).

**British Columbia, Canada**
*   **Guideline**: Set annually, usually tied to inflation (e.g., 3.5%).
*   **Notice Period**: 3 full rental months.
*   **Exemptions**: Non-profit housing, commercial tenancies.
*   **Required Form**: Form RTB-7.

**California, USA (AB 1482)**
*   **Guideline**: 5% + local CPI, or 10%, whichever is lower.
*   **Notice Period**: 30 days (if increase < 10%), 90 days (if increase > 10%).
*   **Exemptions**: Single-family homes/condos (unless corporate-owned), buildings built within the last 15 years.
