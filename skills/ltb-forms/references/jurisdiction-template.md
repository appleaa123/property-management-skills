# Jurisdiction Template: Worked Example (Ontario, Canada)

This document serves as an example of how to structure the requirements for a specific jurisdiction. When operating in a new region, research and create a similar mental map.

## Jurisdiction: Ontario (Landlord and Tenant Board - LTB)

**Common Forms and Triggers:**
*   **N1**: Notice of Rent Increase (Standard). *Trigger*: Annual rent increase within guideline.
*   **N4**: Notice to End a Tenancy Early for Non-payment of Rent. *Trigger*: Tenant is late on rent.
*   **N9**: Tenant's Notice to End the Tenancy. *Trigger*: Tenant wants to move out.
*   **N12**: Notice to End your Tenancy Because the Landlord, a Purchaser or a Family Member Requires the Rental Unit. *Trigger*: Owner moving in.

**Standard Required Fields (All Forms):**
1.  **To (Tenant Names)**: Must include all individuals named on the lease.
2.  **From (Landlord Name)**: Must be the legal entity name.
3.  **Address of the Rental Unit**: Must be exact.

**Specific Form Examples:**

### N4 (Non-payment of Rent)
*   **Specific Fields Required**:
    *   Termination Date (Must be at least 14 days after serving the notice).
    *   Table of Rent Owed (Period from/to, Rent Charged, Rent Paid, Total Rent Owing).
*   **Delivery Rules**: Can be handed to tenant, placed in mailbox, or slid under door. Cannot be emailed unless tenant explicitly consented in writing to email service of legal documents.

### N1 (Rent Increase)
*   **Specific Fields Required**:
    *   Date the increase takes effect (Must be at least 90 days after serving).
    *   New rent amount.
*   **Delivery Rules**: Standard service rules apply.
