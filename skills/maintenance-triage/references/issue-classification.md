# Issue Classification & Urgency Guide

Use these guidelines to classify inbound maintenance requests by trade and determine if they constitute an emergency.

## Urgency: Emergency vs. Routine

**Emergency (Requires Immediate Dispatch - Under 4 hours):**
*   Active flooding, burst pipes, or uncontrollable water leaks.
*   No heat in winter (outside temps below freezing).
*   Gas leaks (instruct tenant to evacuate and call utility immediately).
*   Complete power outage to the entire property.
*   Major structural damage (e.g., collapsed roof).
*   Security breaches (e.g., broken exterior door or lock).

**Routine (Standard Dispatch - 24 to 72 hours):**
*   Dripping faucets, slow drains.
*   Appliance malfunction (fridge, stove, washer).
*   Flickering lights, single dead outlet.
*   Minor drywall damage or paint issues.

## Classification: Required Trade

Map keywords in the tenant's description to the appropriate vendor trade:

*   **Plumbing**: "leak", "flood", "toilet", "sink", "drain", "water heater", "pipe", "no hot water".
*   **Electrical**: "power", "outlet", "breaker", "spark", "lights", "shock".
*   **HVAC (Heating/Cooling)**: "no heat", "furnace", "ac", "air conditioning", "thermostat".
*   **Appliance Repair**: "fridge", "refrigerator", "stove", "oven", "washer", "dryer", "dishwasher".
*   **Roofing**: "roof leak", "shingles", "ceiling leak (top floor)".
*   **General/Handyman**: "door", "window", "drywall", "cabinet", "blind", "lock" (unless security emergency).
