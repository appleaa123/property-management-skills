# Persona Extraction Methodology

This guide explains how to extract a communication style, either from raw data or via direct interview.

## Method A: Data Analysis (Interaction Logs)

When analyzing past messages, look for the following signals:

1.  **Sentence Structure**: Are sentences long and complex, or short and declarative?
2.  **Pronoun Usage**: Does the manager use "I" (personal), "We" (team), or "Management" (institutional)?
3.  **Boundary Setting**: How does the manager say "no"? (e.g., "Unfortunately, we cannot..." vs. "Per section 4 of your lease...")
4.  **Formatting**: Do they use bullet points? Bold text for emphasis? Emojis?

## Method B: The Interview Script

If no data is available, interview the manager. **Do not copy/paste this entire block to the user.** Ask these questions conversationally, 1 or 2 at a time.

**Phase 1: Tone**
1.  "How would you describe your ideal communication style with tenants? (e.g., strictly professional, friendly and casual, firm but fair?)"
2.  "How do you usually start and end your emails?"

**Phase 2: Conflict & Boundaries**
3.  "If a good tenant asks for an extension on rent for the first time, how do you typically respond?"
4.  "If a tenant is aggressively complaining about a minor issue (like a dripping faucet), how do you de-escalate or set boundaries?"

**Phase 3: Operations & Approval**
5.  "For maintenance requests, at what dollar amount do you want to review the quote before the vendor starts work?"
6.  "What types of issues do you consider 'drop everything' emergencies?"
